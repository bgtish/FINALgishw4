Homework 8: Geoprocessing

Bettye Tish

For this map, I sought to answer the question: what areas of Atlanta, Georgia, constitute food deserts? Very quickly in this process, I learned that a food desert must meet two qualifications: a high poverty area and no source of fresh food walking distance. For the purposes of my map, I went by UC Davis' measurement of "poverty areas" (areas with a poverty rate of more than 20%) and the commonly used urban planning measurement of walking distance (1 mile, which is very generous). After cleaning my data sets in QGIS using attribute table queries, I first created a buffer of walking distance around local grocery stores. Then, I created a new layer using the difference geoprocessing tool, finding areas that were both high poverty and not included in the walking distance radius of grocery stores.
